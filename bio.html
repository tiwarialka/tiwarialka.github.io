About Me

I am a postdoc at Jackson School of Geosciences at UT Austin, where I am part of The Extreme weather and Urban Sustainability (TExUS) Lab.
I have received my PhD from Purdue University advised by Dr. Keith Cherkauer and mentored by Dr. Frank Marks, Dr. Wen-wen Tung and Dr. Dev Niyogi as my committee members. I was advised by Dr. Shivam Tripathi for my Master's in Civil Engineering, majoring in Hydraulics and Water Resources Engineering at IIT Kanpur.​

Academically, I have spent about two years teaching as Assitant Professor atthe School of Civil Engineering, KIIT University and being a Project Scientist at Interdisciplinary Centre for Water Research (ICWaR) -IISc Bangalore.

Brief Bio

Alka is a first generation international student and NASA FINESST fellow pursuing her research in the field of hydrology, data science and extreme events such as hurricane.
In her free time, Alka enjoys astronomy, hiking, tennis, mountaineering. She is keen to explore books and conversations that invigorate ideas and perception, practice meditation and is vocal about causes related to women’s representation in data science and engineering.
Alka is born in Ballia, Uttar Pradesh, India and did her schooling in Rajkamal Saraswati Vidya Mandir, Dhanbad, Jharkhand. She has travelled and lived across different states of india, for job, studies and leisure.
